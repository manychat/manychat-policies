# **1. General**

## **1.1 Purpose**

Manychat is responsible for confidentiality, integrity, and availability of its data and data of its customers stored in its systems.

This document describes Manychat's requirements, standards and procedures for retention, handling and disposal of Manychat’s data. This ensures that information is stored, secured, accessible and disposed of in a consistent and timely manner.

## **1.2 Scope**

This policy applies to all teams, divisions, sections and services of Manychat and to all data categories, including those provided for by statute, held by or within Manychat. It includes categories of customer, personal and sensitive data, employee data including contractors, sub-contractors and agency personnel, corporate, ﬁnancial and workforce information where one or more of the following conditions are met:

- The data category is used to meet business, operational or legislative requirements.
- Manychat has a strategic need for the data.
- The data category contains personal, customer or sensitive information.
- The data category contains payment information.
- The data category is used across multiple services.

The scope of this policy includes all books, records and electronic data processed within Manychat.

This policy is not covering disposal/retention of unused or public materials such as public documentation, unused forms, materials or equipment that do not comprise a record of a business activity/transaction, personal or customer data.

# **2. Data Retention Policy**

## **2.1 General requirements**

1. The data must remain available, accessible, retrievable and usable for as long as a business need exists or as long as legislative, regulatory, policy and archival provisions and procedures require them to be kept.
2. Paper-based and electronic information systems should include and apply physical and technical security controls to ensure the integrity, security and availability of information is not compromised. The security controls are described in the [Office Security Standard](https://www.notion.so/Office-Security-Standard-2b7b12e9aa1a8005ab2edd693784d06f?pvs=21)  and [**Infrastructure Security Standard**](https://www.notion.so/Infrastructure-Security-Standard-2c4b12e9aa1a80838307f8869e021025?pvs=21).
3. Storage conditions for paper records should be designed to protect the data not only from unauthorized access and theft, but from damage that can be caused by vermin, ﬁre, water, mold and natural disasters.
4. Disposal of data must be carried out in accordance with the relevant Retention Schedule. A data retention schedule must exist for every data category, deﬁning record type and data retention period, after which time the data is securely disposed of.
5. Conﬁdential information must be destroyed by a method that ensures no possibility of reconstructing the contents.
6. The data disposal methods and processes (automatic or manual) must be executed on a regular basis (daily, weekly, monthly, etc.) to meet compliance; all these activities must be logged within an audit process.

## **2.2 Data Categories and Data Retention Schedules**

A data retention should be deﬁned and exist for each category of data. Data categories are groups of data with common characteristics or features. The data retention and disposal requirements are deﬁned and managed for every category.

The following data categories are deﬁned in Manychat:

- Customer data (including subscribers, Live Chats, and broadcasts)
- Financial data
- Enterprise Management data
- Legal and Regulatory (including customer regulatory) data
- HR data
- Sales data
- Partner and Supplier data
- Product and Technology data
- Marketing data.

### 2.2.1 Customer data

Customer data is disposed of after contract termination or by customer request within 30 days. Otherwise, if the account is deleted within Manychat, all data owned by this account gets automatically disposed of from the Manychat databases in 187 days grace time.

Data disposal requests can be revoked by the customer in a 7-calendar days period.

| **Data Category** | **Data Retention Schedule** |
| --- | --- |
| **Customer data** |  |
| Customer Data (including Live chat and broadcast data) in Production and Analytics Environments of **active** accounts | 1. After [voluntary account deletion within Manychat](https://support.manychat.com/en/support/solutions/articles/36000390813-how-to-delete-an-account) - **30 days** from the deletion request.
2. After [Personal Data Management request](https://manychat.com/resources/manage-my-data) - **within 30 days** from the deletion request.
3. Banned, Suspended or Blocked accounts via admin panel - **187 days** after the event. |
| Customer Data (including Live chat and broadcast data) in Production and Analytics Environments of **inactive** accounts | 18 months after one of the following events (whatever comes later):
• Last login activity date
• Last incoming or outgoing message date
• Last payment date
• Planned broadcast date |

# **3. Roles and Responsibilities**

The CEO has the overall responsibility for Information Security at the Company, including information security regarding personnel and IT security.

Process specific roles and responsibilities:

| **Role** | **Role description** |
| --- | --- |
| **All employees** | Employees are responsible to comply with the Standard and report the violations to the Security team. |
| **Managers** | Line managers are responsible to ensure the awareness of this Standard across their teams. |
| **Security team** | Security team is responsible for monitoring overall awareness with this Standard, monitoring, and investigating the violations, and deviations handling. |
| **Infrastructure team** | Infrastructure team is responsible for customer data retention and disposal in the production environment. |
| **Data engineering team** | Data engineering team is responsible for customer data retention and disposal in the data warehouse environment. |

# **4. Compliance**

The proof of ISMS quality is the receipt and maintenance of the certificate of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 «Information Technology. Security techniques. Information security management systems. Requirements» compliance.

Manychat must comply with multiple state regulatory requirements of countries, Manychat operates in, such as:

- The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (Regulation (EU) 2016/679);
- Personal data protection regulation in the countries or regions of operation (including CCPA and Law of Armenia on protection of personal data ( ЗR-49-N));
- Canada’s Anti-Spam Law (or “CASL”);
- The CAN-SPAM Act;
- other mandatory state regulatory acts including acts on commercial secrets and intellectual property.

## **4.1 Enforcement**

Implementation and enforcement of this policy is ultimately the responsibility of all employees at Manychat. Security team may conduct ad-hoc checks to ensure compliance with this document without notice. Any violation shall require immediate corrective action. Violations shall be noted in the Manychat applications and responsible employees shall be dispatched to remediate the issue.

For Manychat employees, breaches of this policy may result in disciplinary action being taken and will be dealt with under the disciplinary process in accordance with state laws and regulations of the residence country. Any serious breaches or repeated breaches of this policy may be deemed by Manychat as gross misconduct and may result in disciplinary sanctions, including a remark, reprimand, and dismissal from work.

## **4.2 Exceptions**

Exceptions to this standard must be processed in accordance with the deviation handling rules by the Security team. All exceptions must be maintained taking into account associated risks, exceptions, and recorded approvals. Continuously detected deviations may trigger the changes or improvements of the measures defined in this document.
