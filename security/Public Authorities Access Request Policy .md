## **1. Purpose and Scope**

This internal policy outlines the procedures for identifying, evaluating, and responding to:

- Requests from law enforcement or other public authorities (“**Requesting Authorities**”) seeking access to personal data (“**Data Disclosure Requests**”); and
- Instances of **Direct Access**, where a public authority accesses personal data without prior notice or formal request.

The policy also establishes standards for:

- Pre-transfer assessments of third-country access laws
- Notification obligations toward customers and authorities
- Internal accountability and escalation protocols

**Applicability:** This policy applies to all Manychat personnel, regardless of role or department, who may encounter, process, or become aware of public authority requests involving personal data.

---

## **2. Pre-Transfer Legal Assessment (Third-Country Access Risks)**

### **2.1 Legal and Practical Evaluation**

Before transferring personal data to a third country, Manychat will assess the legal and practical risks, including:

- Existence of surveillance or mandatory disclosure laws
- Oversight and redress mechanisms
- Compatibility with applicable data protection standards and fundamental rights

The assessment considers:

- Purpose and context of the transfer
- Data types and sensitivity
- Identity of recipients and transmission mechanisms

### **2.2 Ongoing Monitoring**

Personnel responsible for privacy and compliance must monitor legal developments in relevant countries and update assessments if risks change.

### **2.3 Safeguards and Suspension**

If risks are identified, safeguards (contractual, technical, or organizational) must be applied. If sufficient safeguards are not feasible, the transfer must be suspended. All actions must be documented and made available to supervisory authorities upon request.

---

## **3. General Principle on Government Access**

Manychat does **not** disclose personal data to public authorities unless:

- There is a valid, binding legal obligation (e.g. subpoena, warrant); or
- There is a demonstrable, imminent risk of serious harm that justifies emergency disclosure

Where appropriate, affected customers and supervisory authorities will be notified before disclosure.

---

## **4. Handling Government Data Requests**

### **4.1 Identification and Escalation of Requests**

### **4.1.1 Recognition of a Request**

Requests may be:

- **Formal:** Subpoenas, court orders, warrants, official notices
- **Informal:** Emails, phone calls, web forms, in-person requests

They may seek:

- Customer or user content
- Metadata or account activity
- Identifying information

### **4.1.2 Escalation Procedure**

Upon receiving a request:

1. **Do not engage with the requester or provide any data**
2. **Acknowledge and redirect**
    - Suggested reply:
        
        <aside>
        💡
        
        > *“Manychat has established procedures for handling government and law enforcement requests. Your request will be referred to the appropriate internal function for review.”*
        > 
        </aside>
        
3. **Immediately escalate**
    - 📧 Email: [privacy@manychat.com](mailto:privacy@manychat.com)
    - 🔒 Slack: Please reach out @Mariam Dadunts or @ Mikhail Kurzin
    - Include:
        - Full request and attachments
        - Date/time/method received
        - Requester contact info
4. **If received via ticketing system (e.g., Zendesk)**
    - Assign to Privacy & Security queue
    - Set internal visibility and high priority
5. **Maintain confidentiality**
    - Do not share in external or public systems

---

### **4.2 Legitimacy Assessment**

Personnel responsible for privacy and compliance will assess:

- **Jurisdictional authority** of the requesting entity
- **Legal basis** for the request
- **Specificity and scope** of requested data
- **Purpose and proportionality** of the request
- **Formal service** and identification
- **Compliance risks** under GDPR or contractual obligations
- **Notification and challenge** opportunities

Requests that fail these checks may be denied, narrowed, or formally challenged. All actions must be documented.

---

## **5. Notification of Customers and Authorities**

### **5.1 Customer Notification**

If Manychat processes the data as a **processor**, it will:

- Refer the request to the customer (if possible)
- Notify the customer directly unless:
    - Prohibited by law, or
    - Notification would pose a serious harm risk

Disclosure without customer authorization occurs only when legally required.

### **5.2 Supervisory Authority Consultation**

If the request comes from a jurisdiction without adequate safeguards:

- Notify and consult the relevant data protection authority (unless prohibited)
- Seek to delay or challenge the request where appropriate
- Document all steps taken

---

## **6. Involvement of Service Providers and Sub-Processors**

Manychat uses third-party service providers and sub-processors for operations such as hosting, infrastructure, support, and analytics.

- **Referral Obligation:** All third parties must forward any public authority request involving Manychat data to Manychat.
- **No Independent Disclosure:** Sub-processors may not respond independently unless instructed by Manychat or legally compelled (and permitted to notify us).
- **Oversight:** Manychat evaluates compliance through due diligence and contractual controls.

Any request forwarded by a sub-processor will be handled per Sections 4 and 5 of this policy.

---

## **7. Documentation and Recordkeeping**

All requests must be logged in the **Government Access Request Register**, including:

- Request date and origin
- Legal basis and issuing body
- Data requested and scope
- Actions taken
- Notifications (if any)

The register is maintained securely and made available upon regulatory or audit request.

---

## **8. Prohibited Practices**

The following are strictly prohibited:

- Voluntary disclosure without legal obligation
- Informal responses to public authority requests
- Overbroad or indiscriminate data disclosure
- Circulating government requests via unauthorized systems
