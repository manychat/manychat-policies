# Table of Contents

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to define a structured and consistent approach to **internal and external communication related to the AI Management System (AIMS)** and AI-enabled systems throughout their lifecycle, in accordance with ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 7.4.

This policy ensures that relevant information about AI systems, their operation, risks, changes, and governance is communicated in a timely, appropriate, and coordinated manner.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- AI-enabled features and systems operated within the Manychat platform
- Activities across the AI system lifecycle, including design, integration, deployment, operation, monitoring, and change management
- Internal and external communications related to AI governance, risks, incidents, and material changes

## 3. Communication Objectives

Communication under the AIMS framework aims to:

- Support effective AI governance and informed decision-making
- Ensure appropriate awareness among relevant internal stakeholders
- Enable timely escalation and coordination in case of AI-related risks or incidents
- Support transparency and accountability where communication with external parties is required

## 4. Communication Framework

### 4.1 Audiences

Internal AIMS communication is directed to relevant stakeholders based on the topic and lifecycle stage. Core audiences include:

- **Security (including GRC within Security)**
- **AI system owners and delivery teams** (e.g., Product, Engineering, Data)
- **Legal**
- **Senior management / decision-makers**
- **Other internal stakeholders** as needed (e.g., Support, Customer Success)

External audiences may include customers, business or platform partners, vendors or AI service providers, and regulators or auditors, as applicable.

### 4.2 Topics

Internal and external AIMS communication may cover, as applicable:

- AI system lifecycle decisions and changes
- AI risks, impacts, and assessment outcomes
- AI-related incidents, near misses, escalations, and lessons learned
- Updates to AIMS documentation, controls, and governance processes
- Privacy and data handling topics relevant to AI systems

### 4.3 Channels

Communication is conducted using established organizational channels, including, where appropriate:

- Dedicated Slack channels
- Meetings and workshops
- Internal documentation or issue tracking systems
- Email updates or announcements
- Customer- or partner-facing communication channels, where applicable

## 5. External Communication Coordination

External communication related to AI systems shall be coordinated to ensure accuracy, consistency, and compliance with applicable contractual, legal, and regulatory obligations. Coordination may involve Security (including GRC), Legal, and relevant system owners, depending on the nature of the communication.

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

Roles and responsibilities for AIMS-related communication are defined as follows:

- **Security GRC (Privacy & AI Governance)** defines and maintains AIMS communication expectations, coordinates material AI-related communications, and ensures alignment with privacy and AI governance requirements.
- **AI system owners and delivery teams** identify information that requires communication and escalate it through appropriate channels.
- **Legal** supports review and coordination of external communications where required.
- **Management** provides oversight and decision-making for material AI-related risks, incidents, or changes.

## 7. Review and Maintenance

This policy shall be reviewed periodically and updated as necessary to reflect changes in AI system usage, regulatory or standard requirements, or organizational practices.
