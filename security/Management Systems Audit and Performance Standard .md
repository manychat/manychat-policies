# **1. General**

## **1.1 Purpose**

The purpose of this document is to outline a framework for measuring and reviewing the performance of the  management systems within Manychat, including the Information Security Management System (ISMS) and the Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) as a means of pursuing continual improvement.

There is a risk that without an effective  performance monitoring program negative trends that indicate potential problems could be missed. Such eventualities could result in breaches of information confidentiality, integrity, and availability and lead to financial or reputational loss.

Internal audit program should be established in the Company.

Leadership team should regularly evaluate the  performance and  effectiveness of the Management Systems.

## **1.2 Scope**

This standard applies to all divisions, teams, services and all personnel of Manychat. It also applies to external organizations and their personnel who have been granted access to the Manychat infrastructure and services, as well as all employees, contractors, and third parties involved in AI‑related activities on behalf of Manychat.

The document is reviewed at least annually to ensure it meets the current business requirements of Manychat, partners, customers, and external organizations.

# **2. Standard**

## **2.1 General requirements**

A Performance Monitoring and Measurement program shall be created to check that Management System activities, processes, and controls are appropriate. The program schedule should take into account the results of the previous performance reviews and the need for follow-up reviews.

The effectiveness of Management Systems operational and management processes is assessed using performance indicators to measure if certain performance results are achieved by the evaluated process and demonstrate if the resources used to achieve these results have been used efficiently.

The Monitoring and Measurement Criteria that will be monitored throughout the program to show performance against objectives and risks must be identified annually and updated on a quarterly basis.

Monitoring and Measurement Criteria are analyzed quarterly and performance against targets/objectives is determined. Findings shall be analyzed and where performance targets are not achieved, root causes should be identified. Other possible issues / external influences that might affect future performance targets shall also be analyzed, including:

- Organizational changes;
- Technical changes;
- Operational changes;
- Legislation changes;
- New threats & vulnerabilities;
- Cultural changes;
- Business & market changes.

If for objective reasons, some Monitoring and Measurement Criteria are not fully met, they must be corrected during the next monitoring and measurement criteria review.

Regarding the Management System improvement, all interested parties participate in discussing and establishing Monitoring and Measurement Criteria based on the lessons learned from internal assessments, frequency, and types of identified incidents and risks.

As Management Systems' Monitoring and Measurement Criteria are global company performance frameworks, for a deeper dive into processes and Management Systems' continuous maintenance and improvement additional metrics may be established. They can include both direct (driven by Security) and shared (driven by team/division and governed by Security) responsibilities.

Statistics of performance evaluation are presented to the Leadership Team on bi-annual reviews before planning new Goals. During Management review a decision is made on whether any remediation activities must be transferred into relevant teams' objectives.

The Company continually improves the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the ISMS that is confirmed by the appropriate evidence traceable within the PDCA cycle.

The monitoring and measurement activities also consider AI-related objectives, risks, impacts, incidents, performance of AI-enabled systems, effectiveness of safeguards, and compliance with responsible AI requirements.

## **2.3 Internal audit process description**

### 2.3.1 General

The Company conducts internal audits at planned intervals to obtain and provide information to relevant interested parties on whether the Management System:

- Conforms to the Company’s own requirements and requirements of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 or ISO/IEC 42001;
- Information Security is effectively implemented and maintained;
- Identifies risks and improvement opportunities.
- Evaluates the effectiveness of the Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS), including AI governance, risk and impact management, lifecycle controls, and Annex A controls.

The Management System internal audits are delivered as scheduled with the Management System  Internal Three-Year Audit Program ([**Program of Internal Audits**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1eGltc0E_gg9V3TvuWhR_UnfYBLY1HBFI/edit?gid=1292663259#gid=1292663259)), which:

- Ensures a sufficient level of impartiality and absence of any conflicts of interest, e.g. internal auditors shall not audit their own work;
- Considers the importance of processes/areas under audit and the results of previous audits, including external audits of the Management Systems ;
- Is communicated to all Process and System Owners affected by relevant audits.

Delivery of a Management System internal audit comprised of the following consistent stages:

- Preparation for an internal audit;
- Conducting an internal audit;
- Reporting an internal audit results;
- Following up on an internal audit findings.

Breathily these stages are detailed further in this document.

### 2.3.2 Preparation for an internal audit

Internal Auditor creates a yearly internal audit plan based on the Three-Year Programme, considering the status and importance of the processes concerned, changes affecting the organization, and the results of previous audits.

Processes can be included in the internal audit plan for several reasons:

- Standards demand a certain audit frequency;
- Importance of processes. Business-critical processes can be audited more frequently;
- Status of processes. The process has undergone a significant change or a new process has been implemented;
- The Auditee and/or the management request an audit;
- The effectiveness of the process is considered poor.
- Introduction of new AI-enabled systems, material changes to AI functionality, or significant AI-related incidents or concerns.

Before the audit, the Audit Team aligns with the Auditee on the goals and scope of the audit. Special attention points are identified, and the interviewees are determined.

The Audit Team then schedules the interviews and prepares by desk research: examining relevant documentation and deliverables of the audited process and reviewing the relevant reference standards (as relevant considering the scope of the audit) and procedures.

### 2.3.3 Conducting an internal audit

The Audit Team conducts the audit interviews, documentation review, visual inspection (if applicable) as planned, and records the audit outcome in the **internal audit report.** The deviations, non-conformities, findings, opportunities for improvement, and positive feedback (e.g. best practices) are documented. At the end of each interview, the auditor summarizes the deviations, non-conformities, and findings and verifies whether these are formulated correctly.

### 2.3.4 Reporting an internal audit

The Audit Team presents the audit report to the auditee and provides a detailed explanation of its content. The internal audit report is finalized and archived.

### 2.3.5 Follow-up on internal audit

Define/execute/verify preventive and or corrective actions. The internal audit report serves as a starting point for the Auditee and the stakeholders involved in the process to define preventive and corrective actions. These actions are included in the **Corrective Action Plan** as part of the **Deviation handling list**. For more details see section 3.6 (Deviation handling) of this document.

### 2.3.5 Competence

It is expected that persons performing the role of Internal Auditor have the necessary knowledge and skills to deliver the ISMS internal audits, including but not beyond ISO 19011, ISO 27007, and ISO/IEC TR 27008.

## **2.4 Independent reviews**

### 2.4.1 General requirements

External independent reviews are necessary to ensure the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the organization’s approach to managing information security. The review should include assessing opportunities for improvement and the need for changes to the security approach, including the policy and control objectives.

Such a review should be carried out by individuals independent of the area under review, e.g. an independent manager or an external party organization specializing in such reviews. Individuals carrying out these reviews should have the appropriate skills and experience.

The results of the independent review should be recorded and reported to the management who initiated the review. These records should be maintained.

If the independent review identifies that the Company’s approach and implementation to managing information security are inadequate, e.g. documented objectives and requirements are not met or not compliant with the direction for information security stated in the information security policies, management should consider corrective actions (for more details see section 3.6 (deviation handling) of this document).

There are different types of Independent reviews that are detailed further in this document.

### 2.4.2 External audits

External audits or any other type of Company evaluation where one of the parties (auditor or auditee) is not Manychat.

Manychat defines the following types of external audits:

1. **Third-Party audit**, when Manychat engages any 3rd party to ensure its conformity or compliance with specific audit criteria. Such audits include certification audits and other voluntary assessments.
2. **Vendor assessment (or second-party audits)**, when Manychat may conduct an assessment/audit of its vendors based on Vendor assessment methodology to confirm vendor reliability.
3. **Due diligence verification** – where Manychat provides its customers or other interested parties with the possibility to perform an audit of Manychat processes or applications (e.g. penetration testing) on Manychat side to confirm the Company’s compliance with applicable legislation and/or contractual requirements. The terms and conditions of such inspections must be stipulated in the agreements with customers or agreed upon for each specific case after negotiation.
4. **Supervisory inspection** – may happen based on legislative requirements and is usually conducted by a Supervisory authority, other authorized body (e.g. government body), or by any 3rd party on their behalf.
5. **Penetration testing** - when Manychat engages any 3rd party to run external penetration tests against Manychat assets, products or services.

### 2.4.3 Technical audits

Manychat Information systems in accordance with asset inventory should be regularly reviewed for compliance with the Company’s information security policies and standards. Technical compliance reviews involve the examination of operational systems to ensure that hardware and software controls have been correctly implemented. Compliance reviews also cover, for example, penetration testing and vulnerability assessments, which might be carried out by independent experts specifically contracted for this purpose.

In the case of network security analysis using specialized software (scanners), Security Administrators prepare scan reports. The scanning must be coordinated with the Head of Security.

Examples of sources of information about possible vulnerabilities and available updates are:

- websites and newsletters of software manufacturers,
- mailings on IS from Manychat partners,
- news sites and third-party mailings,
- results of the use of specialized software (vulnerability scanners, penetration tests, etc.),
- specialized vulnerability databases like https://cve.mitre.org/, https://www.cvedetails.com/, etc.,
- messages about available software updates,
- other notifications of vulnerabilities, updates, or threats,
- data of regular monitoring of the operation of the asset.

Vulnerability management should be conducted based on the [**Vulnerability and Patch Management Standard**](https://www.notion.so/Vulnerability-and-Patch-Management-Standard-2bfb12e9aa1a802e84baee6fe8c2d058?pvs=21)**.**

Actions to conduct technical audits using technical means must be carefully planned and agreed upon with the owners of the audited assets in order to minimize the risk of failures in the operations and Manychat business processes. Such tests should be planned, documented, and repeatable.

This type of compliance review requires specialist technical expertise.

### 2.4.4 Compliance audits

Compliance with security policies and standards should be regularly reviewed within all processes to ensure that information security is implemented and operated in accordance with the organizational policies and procedures and to assess the level of IS standard implementation within all company units.

Line managers should review within their area of responsibility that information security requirements defined in policies, standards, and other applicable regulations are met (e.g. via team meetings, KPI, tasks, and so on).

If any non-compliance is found as a result of the review, managers should consider corrective actions (for more details see section 3.6 (deviation handling) of this document).

## **2.5 Management review process description**

Leadership team reviews the Management Systems at planned intervals to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness. The Management Systems'  reviews are conducted **bi-annually**.

The following participants are normally taking part in the Management System  reviews:

- Leadership team representative (mandatory);
- Head of Security (mandatory);
- Internal Auditors of the Management System (mandatory);
- Process Owners of the processes within the ISMS and AIMS scope (optional);
- System Owners of the information systems within the ISMS and AIMS scope (optional);
- Other Company’s personnel as necessary (optional).

Each of the  Management System reviews includes consideration of the following:

- Statuses of actions from previous management reviews;
- Changes in external and internal issues that are relevant to the Management System;
- Feedbacks on the information security performance, including trends in nonconformities and corrective actions, monitoring and measurement results, audit results, and fulfillment of information security objectives;
- Feedbacks from interested parties;
- Results of information security risk assessment and status of the risk treatment plan;
- Opportunities for continual improvement.
- Status of AI objectives and progress against responsible AI goals;
- Results of AI risk and impact assessments and AI-related incidents or concerns;
- Changes in legal, regulatory, or contractual requirements related to AI.

The Management System  review outputs include decisions related to continual improvement opportunities and any needs for changes to the Management System.

The Company retains documented information as evidence of the results of management reviews (as minutes meetings, presentations, reports, and other forms, commonly used in the company).

## **2.6 Deviation handling process description**

### 2.6.1 General

Deviation is an apparent or actual difference from a defined/agreed requirement. Possible types of deviations may be findings, customer response, non-conformity, compliance violation, incident, exceptions, and so on. The potential deviation is a possible difference from a specified/agreed requirement.

Approved and reasonable exceptions to any company policy, procedure or standard must be filed as a deviation with all required steps detailed below in this Standart.

The general principle of deviation handling is that each deviation has to be reviewed and its root causes identified, so adequate corrective actions are to be taken in order that it does not recur or occur elsewhere.

Continuous improvement of the ISMS is achieved through the timely development and implementation of Corrective Actions.

### 2.6.2 Reporting deviation

In any case, it is necessary to define the further operation by means of an entry in the [**Deviation handling list**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1lHFWCGU-YPUKWkCddGDr3vuGa6U64Pft/edit?gid=47940169#gid=47940169) available for the area as well as a classification of it. In the case of classification "Critical", an initiation of a **deviation report** as part of the [**Deviation handling list**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1lHFWCGU-YPUKWkCddGDr3vuGa6U64Pft/edit?gid=47940169#gid=47940169) is obligatory, the deviation shall be documented accordingly in each case. Whenever there is the need to include the customer in the process, customers have to be informed about the results of the investigations.

The deviation may arise from the following processes:

- Internal audits of the Management System;
- Any type of independent reviews;
- Monitoring, measurement, analysis, and evaluation of the Management System;
- The Management System  reviews;
- Information security incident management;
- Risk assessment;
- Day-to-day business process activities (exceptions to any company policy, procedure or standard).

Deviation initiator may be:

- One who discovers a potential deviation;
- One who raises a complaint or objection;
- Regulators or authority;
- Internal auditor;
- Employees (e.g. Line managers, process participants);
- One who requests exceptions to any company policy, procedure, or standard.

Any other deviations or potential deviations may be brought forward by anyone at any time as soon as they are discovered or there is the possibility of a deviation.

- Findings may occasionally relate to a specific provision of service and/or general, sometimes, systematic deviations in the internal regulations and procedures;
- Potential deviations relate to potential risks in a process or during service provision.

### 2.6.3 Determining causes of deviation and definition of actions

It is necessary to determine the causes of registered deviation, assess associated risks and opportunities, and define the required corrective actions.

When any deviation occurs, Manychat should:

- React to the deviation in accordance with the type, and as applicable, takes action to control and corrects it deals with the consequences;
- Evaluate the need for action to eliminate the causes of a deviation in order that it does not recur or occur elsewhere by:
    - Reviewing the deviation,
    - Determining the causes of the deviation (e.g. “Five whys” method or Ishikawa-Fishbone diagram), an investigation should be recorded in [**Deviation handling list**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1qGgmNBspc9bhuM5AwmixQwNAYFouIlon/edit#gid=47940169suhDJe4ymx5WQ5McwXgLGk47sl6eWr/edit#gid=47940169),
    - Determining if similar deviations exist, or could potentially occur.
- Implement any needed action appropriating to the effects of the deviations encountered;
- Review the effectiveness of any corrective action taken;
- Make changes to the ISMS and AIMS, if necessary.

### 2.6.4 Implementing corrective actions

For each identified deviation, the responsible should plan corrective actions. It could be one or more corrective action(s), each with an implementation timeframe and an owner -in most cases the manager responsible for the affected area. The Corrective Action owner and target end date are added to the **deviation report** as part of the [**Deviation handling list**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1qGgmNBspc9bhuM5AwmixQwNAYFouIlon/edit#gid=47940169suhDJe4ymx5WQ5McwXgLGk47sl6eWr/edit#gid=47940169).

Once corrective actions are planned and when required approved by the accountable, they are presented to the Leadership team and approved prior to any implementation efforts to be undertaken.

Manychat follows the approach when corrective actions are aligned to the effect given by a deviation.

Head of security may engage any of the Company’s employees, including Process and System Owners, as required for the implementation of the Management System corrective actions and other improvements planned. When the ISMS corrective actions or other planned improvements do not seem possible to be implemented by the Company’s resources, professional services of external origin may be considered (e.g. consultancy services). A person or a group of persons responsible for the implementation of the Management System corrective actions or other planned improvements shall implement them as planned, reporting the implementation status to the Head of Security when demanded.

Corrective actions addressing Critical deviations prevail upon those of Moderate ones in terms of priority in their planning and implementation.

Action owners maintain the status of the implementation of the corrective actions from time to time by updating the status field in the **deviation report** as part of the [**Deviation handling list**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1qGgmNBspc9bhuM5AwmixQwNAYFouIlon/edit#gid=479401694ymx5WQ5McwXgLGk47sl6eWr/edit#gid=47940169).

### 2.6.5 Review and Escalation

The accountable person (e.g. internal auditor) will periodically review the Corrective Actions and assess the effectiveness of the actions. In case deviations were not satisfactorily eliminated or reoccurred, the owner's action must be requested to develop a more permanent solution.

The accountable person should periodically review the Corrective Actions with ‘Due dates’ in the past and remind action owners. After two (2) unsuccessful reminders, the accountable person should escalate the case to the CEO.

The effectiveness of corrective actions taken is assessed during the follow-up ISMS audits as a mandatory part.

During the course of the next Management System internal audit, Internal Auditor reviews the effectiveness of any corrective actions taken since the previous internal audit, by proceeding with different random representative samplings to verify the same deviations do not recur.

If confirmed they do not recur, the appropriate corrective actions are considered effective and relevant deviations are to be closed. Otherwise, the corrective actions are considered not effective, and therefore other corrective actions shall be implemented for the same deviations. Corresponding records upon this process are made on the **Management System Internal Audit Report and deviation report** as part of the [**Deviation handling list**](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1qGgmNBspc9bhuM5AwmixQwNAYFouIlon/edit#gid=47940169x5WQ5McwXgLGk47sl6eWr/edit#gid=47940169).

# **4. Compliance**

The quality and effectiveness of Manychat’s management systems are demonstrated through the establishment, implementation, maintenance, and continual improvement of integrated management systems aligned with recognized international standards.

Manychat maintains an Information Security Management System (ISMS) aligned with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 and an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) aligned with ISO/IEC 42001:2023. These management systems provide a structured framework for managing information security risks as well as risks and impacts arising from the use of AI-enabled systems.

Manychat must comply with applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements in the jurisdictions where it operates, including:

- Data protection and privacy laws (e.g., GDPR; applicable US state privacy laws such as CCPA/CPRA; Brazil LGPD);
- Marketing and anti-spam regulations (e.g., CAN-SPAM Act; CASL, where applicable);
- AI-related regulatory and governance requirements applicable to the use and operation of AI systems (e.g., EU AI Act, where applicable);
- Other mandatory requirements, including commercial secrets and intellectual property regulations.

## **4.1 Enforcement**

Implementation and enforcement of this policy is ultimately the responsibility of all employees at Manychat. Security team may conduct ad-hoc checks to ensure compliance with this document without notice. Any violation shall require immediate corrective action. Violations shall be noted in the Manychat applications and responsible employees shall be dispatched to remediate the issue.

For Manychat employees, breaches of this policy may result in disciplinary action being taken and will be dealt with under the disciplinary process in accordance with state laws and regulations of the residence country. Any serious breaches or repeated breaches of this policy may be deemed by Manychat as gross misconduct and may result in disciplinary sanctions, including a remark, reprimand, and dismissal from work.

## **4.2 Exceptions**

Exceptions to this standard must be processed in accordance with the deviation handling rules by the Security team. All exceptions must be maintained taking into account associated risks, exceptions, and recorded approvals. Continuously detected deviations may trigger the changes or improvements of the measures defined in this document.
